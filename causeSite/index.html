Global Warming What is it?

 
 
 
According to Google, global warming is defined as: "a gradual increase in the overall temperature of the earth's atmosphere generally attributed to the greenhouse effect caused by increased levels of carbon dioxide, chlorofluorocarbons, and other pollutants."

Carbon Dioxide Release

Atmoshpheric concentration of carbon dioxide, the most important "forcing" of climate change, has increased by more than a third since the Industrial Revolution began, according to NASA.

Soil Cultivation

Soil cultivation practices, especially the use of commercial and organic fertilizers, fossil fuel combustion, nitric acid production, and biomass burning all lead to the increased release of Nitric Oxide.

Burning of Oil

Everyday activites, such as driving cars, have allowed for increases in pollution in the atmosphere. It is estimated with more than 90% probability that human activity is to blame for climate change in the past 50 years.
