Effects of Global Warming What are the consequences?

 
"Global sea level has risen by about 8 inches since reliable record keeping began in 1880. It is projected to rise another 1 to 4 feet by 2100. This is the result of added water from melting land ice and the expansion of seawater as it warms," (nasa.gov).
"Droughts in the Southwest and heat waves (periods of abnormally hot weather lasting days to weeks) everywhere are projected to become more intense, and cold waves less intense everywhere," (nasa.gov).
According to nrdc.org, "a 2015 study showed that vertebrate species—animals with backbones, like fish, birds, mammals, amphibians, and reptiles—are disappearing 114 times faster than they should be, a phenomenon that has been linked to climate change, pollution, and deforestation."
Global Warming has affected our planet in several different ways.

Global Warming Brings Higher Death Rates

Today's scientists label climate change as "the biggest global health threat of the 21st century," (nrdc.org). As temperature increase, so do incidence of illness, emergency room visits, and ultimately death. Considering the fact that heat-related deaths occur more frequently than natural disasters, it's safe to say rising temperatures pose a huge threat.
