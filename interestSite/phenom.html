Phenomenological

  Kurt Lewin's Field Theory states that the way in which an object is perceived depends on the total context or arrangement of its surroundings. His theory gave way to experimenal social psychology as it defined behavior as a function of not only the person but also the current psychological environment.
    He called this area of person and environment the "Life Space", the totality of facts that determine behavior of one at a specific moment. This space only takes into account that which is contemporary and disregards past and future. All that matters is the present.

  Abraham Maslow, a phenomenologist, was committed to the study of optimal man and to discover the qualities of those that seem closes to self-actualization, the ultimate goal of each person according to him. The pyramid above puts together Maslow's hierarchy of needs, which calls for the need of self-actualization but can not be reached without saisfying the lower growth needs.
    According to Phenomenological-Humanistic orientation, healthy people who have reached the final peak have become aware of themselves, are focused on the present, have realized their potentialities.

  George Kelly's Personal Construct Theory aimed to see the ways a person aligns events and his/her surroundings on his/her own dimensions. According to Kelly, different people may construe the same event diffferently and every event can be construed in alternative ways.
    He elaborates on this thought by emphasizing that just how a scientiest studies people, these participants also construe behavior by interpreting an judging themselves and surroundings. Therefore, in order to understand a participant in an experiment, a researcher must understnand his/her own constructs and theories.
